## DurajGiesecke\_2025

### Deep Learning for Corporate Bonds

**Author(s):** Jetlir Duraj, Oliver Giesecke\
**Journal / Year:** Working paper

### 1. Summary

* The paper estimates neural-network-based asset pricing models for the U.S. corporate bond market.
*   The SDF is modeled as a tradable portfolio:

    $$
    M_{t+1}=1-\omega_t^\top R^e_{t+1},
    $$

    where the portfolio weights $$\omega_t$$ are generated by neural networks using conditioning information.
*   The paper compares two theoretically related but empirically different objectives:

    $$
    \text{MP-min: minimize mispricing loss}
    $$

    and

    $$
    \text{SR-max: maximize the Sharpe ratio of the SDF portfolio}.
    $$
* Main empirical result:
  * For **individual bonds**, Sharpe-ratio maximization performs better in terms of out-of-sample Sharpe ratios and alphas.
  * Mispricing-loss minimization performs better in terms of explained variation and cross-sectional pricing fit.
  * For **bond portfolios**, the difference between the two objectives is smaller.

### 2. Backdrops

* Most fixed-income empirical asset pricing work focuses on Treasury bonds, such as Cochrane and Piazzesi, Ludvigson and Ng, and Bianchi, Büchner, and Tamoni.
* Corporate bonds are economically large, but harder to study because:
  * individual bond data are less standardized,
  * the panel is highly unbalanced,
  * idiosyncratic noise is large,
  * issuer-level fundamentals must be matched manually to bond-level securities.
* The paper contributes by constructing a large corporate bond dataset that combines:
  * individual bond returns,
  * bond characteristics,
  * issuer fundamentals,
  * equity characteristics,
  * FRED-MD macro variables,
  * Goyal-Welch predictors.
* The authors study both:
  * **40 characteristic-sorted bond portfolios**, and
  * **individual corporate bonds**.

### 3. Framework / Modeling

*   The starting point is the no-arbitrage Euler equation:

    $$
    E_t[M_{t+1}R^e_{t+1}]=0.
    $$
*   The SDF is restricted to be a traded payoff:

    $$
    M_{t+1}=1-\omega_t^\top R^e_{t+1}.
    $$
*   The risk factor implied by the SDF portfolio is:

    $$
    F_{t+1}=\omega_t^\top R^e_{t+1}.
    $$
*   The model learns conditional portfolio weights:

    $$
    \omega_t=\omega(z_t;\theta),
    $$

    where $$z_t$$ includes bond characteristics, issuer characteristics, and macro variables.
*   Under the asset-pricing relation:

    $$
    E_t[R^e_{t+1}]=\beta_t\lambda_t,
    $$

    the pricing residual is written as:

    $$
    \hat{\varepsilon}_{t+1}
    =
    R^e_{t+1}-\hat{R}^e_{t+1}.
    $$
*   The paper’s mispricing-loss objective is:

    $$
    \frac{1}{N}
    \sum_{i=1}^{N}
    \frac{|T_i|}{T}
    \left\|
    \frac{1}{|T_i|}
    \sum_{t\in T_i}
    \left(
    1-\omega(z_t;\theta)^\top R^e_{t+1}
    \right)
    R^e_{t+1,i}
    \right\|^2.
    $$
*   This objective directly penalizes deviations from:

    $$
    E[M_{t+1}R^e_{t+1,i}]=0.
    $$
*   The alternative objective uses the SDF portfolio return:

    $$
    R^{SDF}_{t+1}
    =
    \omega(z_t;\theta)^\top R^e_{t+1}.
    $$
*   Instead of the fractional Sharpe ratio during training, the paper uses a linearized Sharpe-ratio objective:

    $$
    SR
    =
    \operatorname{mean}_t
    \left(
    \omega(z_t;\theta)^\top R^e_{t+1}
    \right)
    -
    \gamma
    \operatorname{stdev}_t
    \left(
    \omega(z_t;\theta)^\top R^e_{t+1}
    \right).
    $$
* The authors use $$\gamma=0.01$$.
* Model architectures:
  * shallow feed-forward neural network,
  * deeper feed-forward neural network,
  * even deeper feed-forward neural network,
  * elastic-net-like one-layer architecture.
*   The weights are normalized using an $$L^2$$ norm constraint to control leverage:

    $$
    \|\omega\|_2 \leq C.
    $$
*   For bond portfolios:

    $$
    C_{\text{port}}=1.
    $$
*   For individual bonds:

    $$
    C_{\text{bonds}}=0.1.
    $$
*   Predictive performance is evaluated using explained variation:

    $$
    EV
    =
    1-
    \frac{
    \frac{1}{T}\sum_{t=1}^T
    \operatorname{crosssec\text{-}mean}(\varepsilon_t^2)
    }{
    \frac{1}{T}\sum_{t=1}^T
    \operatorname{crosssec\text{-}mean}((R_t^e)^2)
    }.
    $$
*   Cross-sectional $$R^2$$ is:

    $$
    XS\text{-}R^2
    =
    1-
    \frac{
    \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}
    \frac{T_i}{T}
    \left(
    \operatorname{mean}_t(\varepsilon_i)
    \right)^2
    }{
    \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}
    \frac{T_i}{T}
    \left(
    \operatorname{mean}_t(R_i)
    \right)^2
    }.
    $$

### 4. Data / Results

* The individual bond dataset contains:
  * 38,955 unique securities before final filtering,
  * 7,995 unique issuers before final filtering,
  * 2,341,941 non-missing monthly return observations.
* The final individual bond estimation dataset contains:
  * 10,715 securities,
  * 1,922 issuers,
  * sample period from February 1973 to January 2020.
* The bond portfolio dataset contains:
  * 40 characteristic-sorted corporate bond portfolios,
  * 126 FRED-MD macro variables,
  * 6 Goyal-Welch variables,
  * 41 median issuer-fundamental time series,
  * sample period from October 1978 to December 2018.
* For bond portfolios:
  * best monthly Sharpe ratio is about $$0.25$$,
  * annualized Sharpe ratio is about $$0.87$$,
  * models with macro variables generally have better Sharpe ratios and lower drawdowns.

* For individual bonds:
  * the best monthly Sharpe ratio is $$0.29$$,
  * annualized Sharpe ratio is about $$1.00$$,
  * Sharpe-ratio maximization dominates mispricing-loss minimization in investment performance.

### 5. Contribution

* The paper extends neural-network SDF estimation to the U.S. corporate bond market.
* It constructs a large individual corporate bond panel linked to issuer fundamentals and macroeconomic data.
*   It compares two theoretically connected SDF estimation criteria:

    $$
    E[M_{t+1}R^e_{t+1}]=0
    $$

    versus

    $$
    \max SR(\omega_t^\top R^e_{t+1}).
    $$
* The main empirical contribution is that these two objectives are not equivalent in finite-sample, noisy, high-dimensional corporate bond data.
* For individual bonds:
  * Sharpe-ratio maximization gives better tradable SDF portfolio performance.
  * Mispricing-loss minimization gives better pricing and predictive fit.
* The paper shows that nonlinear neural-network SDF models outperform conventional linear pricing-kernel models using macro factors and the Cochrane-Piazzesi factor.
* The paper also studies a GAN-style SDF architecture related to Chen, Pelger, and Zhu, but finds that it suffers from mode collapse for individual bonds under their limited hyperparameter search.
